Learn about blood Donation


The human body contains five liters of blood, which is made of several useful components i.e. Whole blood, Platelet, and Plasma. Each type of component has several medical uses and can be used for different medical treatments. your blood donation determines the best donation for you to make.
For plasma and platelet donation you must have donated whole blood in past two years.

Why Donate blood online


  • Improved efficiency and accuracy: E-blood banks use technology to automate many of the tasks involved in blood bank management, such as blood donor screening, blood collection, blood testing, and blood inventory management. This can help to improve the efficiency and accuracy of blood banking operations.
  • Increased transparency: E-blood banks can provide real-time information about blood availability and blood donor eligibility to patients and healthcare professionals. This can help to increase transparency and improve the decision-making process when it comes to blood transfusions.
  • Better accessibility: E-blood banks can make it easier for patients and healthcare professionals to access blood and blood products, regardless of their location. For example, patients can use e-blood banks to find blood donors in their area or to request blood deliveries.
  • Reduced costs: E-blood banks can help to reduce the costs associated with blood banking by automating tasks and streamlining processes. This can make blood more affordable for patients and healthcare providers.
  • Simple and Fast User Experience.

  • Whole Blood Separation

    • After donation, the whole blood is typically separated into three components: red blood cells, plasma, and platelets. Each of these components plays a crucial role in treating different medical conditions.

  • Red Blood Cells

    • These cells carry oxygen throughout the body and are often used in treatments for anemia, surgery, trauma, cancer, and diseases of the blood.

  • Plasma

    • This is the liquid part of the blood that carries cells and proteins throughout the body. It is used in treatments for burn victims, shock, and patients with clotting disorders.
